Movie, Book, and Album Recommendations

Books

Fergus Crane - Paul Stewart

Fergus Crane was my favorite childhood story. It follows Fergus, a boy living on an island who is whisked away on an exciting adventure. The book features illustrations by Chris Riddell, who has an incredibly unqiue style. His drawings paired with a relatively mature and engaging story made this book stick in my mind as a child. Even today I remember many of the illustrations and could regonize his style anywhere. This book captivated me in my youth, and I would reccomend it for anyone with children interested in reading.

House of Leaves - Mark Z. Danielewski

This is the book I am currently reading, and I love it so far! The story is about a young man, Johnny, who finds hundreds pages of writing about a movie that never existed. The book details these pages with footnotes from Johnny. As the plot of the movie becomes more supernatural, the footnates become less grounded, and Johnny begins to ramble. It is an incredibly engaging postmodern story, and I look forward to finishing it!

Movies

The Princess Bride (1987)

This was my favorite movie as a child, and remains that way today. The humor is entertaining for all ages, and the actors are flawless. In addition, many lines are iconic pieces of recent culture, and many people quote it without even realizing. Overall, it is a great movie with undeniable impact.

Everything Everywhere All At Once (2022)

I watched this movie over the Summer, a year after it was first released. The story is simultaneously emotional and hilarious, and I had to frequently hold myself back from laughing or crying too loud. In addition, it features many practical effects as a result of its lower budget. Many large movie studios are afraid to experiment in the same way that this movie did, and I hope its success inspires more original movies.

Albums

Glass Houses - Billy Joel (1980)

After listening to this album a couple years ago, I quickly fell in love. Every song is great at minimum, if not perfect. They are all catchy, memorable, and great for any road trip playlist. My favorite songs are Sleeping With the Television On and You May be Right.

Aim and Ignite - fun. (2009)

Like many, I discovered fun. through their second album, Some Nights. But after listening to the rest of their music, I began to prefer their first album. Some Nights is a great album, but it is more suited to be played on a radio, losing much of the unique sound featured in Aim and Ignite. There are some songs that I would rather skip, but highlights like Benson Hedges and Light A Roman Candle With Me make up for it.
